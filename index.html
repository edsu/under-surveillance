[Untitled]. 2001. September 11 Digital Archive.
I'm not sure that this work would have resonated with people if it hadn't been for CHNM's work on the September 11 Digital Archive. The idea that the Web is an integral part of our understanding our cultural moment, and the work of history is something that the 9/11 Archive helped us realize as a community. Also, the idea that it is in momemnts of profound loss and destruction that we find the means to connect with each other to help build collective memory. An idea reminiscent of Rebecca Solnit's A Paradise Built in Hell. Omeka in particular is an important bit of plumbing in that realization. This is something that I'm going to return to near the end.

Gellman, B. and Poitras, L. "U.S., British intelligence mining data from nine U.S. Internet companies in broad secret program." Washington Post. June 7, 2013.
In many ways these tools developed by the NSA and CIA speak to the archival imaginary. But these powerful organizations don't need us to develop tools. However citizen archivists and others do.

Quinn, Zoe. Photograph of Zoe Quinn with camera, taken in 2014
Gamergate harrassment of individuals such as Zoe Quinn, Anita Sarkeesian, Brianna Wu. Small number of individuals amplifying their voices online to harras and doxx women online. While we want to build tools that empower people working for public memory to document events, we don't want to empower bad actors.
